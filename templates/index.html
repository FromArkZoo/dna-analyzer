Health Risk Variants

These are genetic variants in your DNA that have been linked to health conditions in published medical research. Each card shows a gene, the associated condition, and how significant the finding is.

What the severity levels mean:
  • Critical Variants strongly linked to serious conditions — discuss with your doctor
  • High Notable risk elevation or important carrier status
  • Moderate Modest risk increase — worth being aware of
  • Low Minor risk factor — common in the general population
  • Protective Good news! This variant is associated with reduced risk

Zygosity: "Heterozygous" means you carry one copy of the variant (usually lower impact). "Homozygous" means you carry two copies (usually higher impact).

🔍
⚠ These results are based on published research associations and do not constitute a diagnosis. A consumer DNA test only checks a tiny fraction of your genome — a "clear" result does not mean zero risk.

Pharmacogenomics (Drug Sensitivities)

Your genes affect how your body processes medications. Some people break down drugs faster or slower than average, which can make a standard dose too strong, too weak, or even dangerous.

What the metabolizer types mean:
  • Poor Metabolizer — Your body breaks down the drug very slowly. Standard doses may build up and cause stronger side effects.
  • Intermediate Metabolizer — Somewhat slower processing. You may need a lower dose than typical.
  • Normal Metabolizer — You process the drug as expected. Standard dosing is likely appropriate.
  • Rapid / Ultra-rapid Metabolizer — Your body breaks down the drug very quickly. Standard doses may be less effective, or in some cases (like codeine), dangerously converted to active forms.
🔍
⚠ Never change or stop medications based on this report. Always discuss with your physician or pharmacist first.

Ancestry & Origins

Your DNA carries traces of your ancient ancestors' migrations. Haplogroups are deep ancestral lineages passed down through your mother (mtDNA) and father (Y-DNA, males only). These trace back thousands of years and tell you about the geographic origins of your direct maternal and paternal lines.

⚠ Ancestry estimates are rough approximations from a limited set of markers. They may differ significantly from other services that use more advanced methods. Think of these as broad strokes, not a detailed portrait.
